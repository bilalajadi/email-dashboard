Deliverability Metrics
These metrics determine if AWS will suspend your sending. Keep bounce and complaint rates low to maintain good sender reputation.
Source: AWS SES via SNS webhooks ‚Üí Supabase email_events table
Bounce Rate --
--%
Emails that failed to deliver - usually because the email address doesn't exist or the inbox is full. High bounce rate means you're sending to bad addresses.
AWS Thresholds: Safe <2% | Warning 5% | Suspension 10%
Complaint Rate --
--%
People who clicked "Mark as Spam" on your email. This is the most damaging metric - it tells email providers your emails are unwanted.
AWS Thresholds: Safe <0.05% | Warning 0.08% | Suspension 0.1%
Open Rate --
--%
People who opened your email. Tracked via a tiny invisible image. Shows if your subject lines are working and if people care about your emails.
Industry benchmarks: Good >25% | Average 15-25% | Low <15%
Click Rate --
--%
People who clicked a link in your email. Shows if your content is compelling enough to drive action.
Industry benchmarks: Good >3% | Average 1-3% | Low <1%
DNS Authentication
These are security records in your domain's DNS that prove your emails are really from you (not a scammer pretending to be you). Without them, your emails will go to spam.
Source: Live DNS lookups via Google Public DNS
SPF
--
Sender Policy Framework - A list of servers allowed to send emails from your domain. Like a "guest list" at the door.
If missing: Emails may be rejected or marked as spam
DKIM
--
DomainKeys Identified Mail - A digital signature attached to every email proving it wasn't changed in transit. Like a tamper-proof seal.
If missing: Emails look suspicious and may be flagged
DMARC
--
Domain-based Message Authentication - Tells email providers what to do with emails that fail SPF/DKIM checks (reject, quarantine, or allow).
If missing: You lose control over fake emails using your domain
